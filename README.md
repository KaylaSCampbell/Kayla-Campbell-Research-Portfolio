# Research Portfolio — Kayla Campbell

Mathematics student at Spelman College specializing in quantitative modeling, geospatial analysis, and urban infrastructure systems. This portfolio highlights applied research in transportation accessibility and housing market dynamics, with a focus on infrastructure equity, spatial efficiency, and data-driven decision support for urban and real estate systems.

---

## Network Analysis of the 15-Minute City Model in Atlanta's West End
**Spelman College | Spelman-Morehouse Directed Reading Program | Fall 2024**

Applied network theory and graph-based mathematical modeling using OSMnx, 
Python, and GIS to evaluate pedestrian accessibility and infrastructure 
connectivity in Atlanta's West End neighborhood. Research produced actionable 
recommendations for transit integration, pedestrian pathway improvements, 
and equitable service distribution to support sustainable urban development.

[View Poster](urban-infrastructure-15min-city-spelman.pdf)

---

## Housing Justice Atlas: Rent Exploitation, Rent Burden, and Residential Segregation
**University of Virginia School of Data Science | Summer 2022**

Analyzed housing and demographic data across 317 census tracts in 
Charlottesville and Richmond, VA using the 2020 American Community Survey. 
Applied regression analysis and statistical modeling in R to evaluate 
relationships between race, poverty, rent burden, and residential segregation. 
Research was funded by Capital One and conducted through the UVA Data Justice 
Academy.

[View Poster](housing-justice-atlas-uva.pdf)
